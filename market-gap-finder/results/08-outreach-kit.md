# Outreach Kit (fence / welding / contractor beachhead)

_Honest, non-spammy cold outreach for local contractors with strong reviews but a weak or broken online presence. Worked example: Weldz-R-Us (Houston welder/fabricator: 93 Google reviews, 22 yrs, veteran-owned, website weldz-r-us.com is DOWN, FB last post about 16 mo ago)._

**Rules (keep it clean):**
- Never claim a false gap. Verify each prospect on Maps and click their website before you send. (The scan said Weldz-R-Us had "no website"; really it's a dead site, which is a very different pitch.)
- One clear ask per message. Lead with a helpful observation, not a sales pitch.
- Always give an easy opt-out on email. No fake urgency, no invented stats.
- Don't promise guaranteed rankings. Promise setup, management, and more visibility.

## 4. The free 1-page audit (what you send / show to close)

One page, 5 blocks. This IS the product demo, so do it well and the sale is halfway made.

1. **Search visibility:** a screenshot of the Google map for "[service] [city]" showing they're NOT in the top 3 (and who is).
2. **Profile scorecard vs. one competitor:** a tiny table of reviews, photos, last post date, website working, correct category. Theirs vs. the competitor beating them.
3. **The broken or missing website:** screenshot of the dead page or the empty "website" slot on their profile.
4. **3 fixes that move the needle in about 30 days:** (a) fix/replace website link, (b) correct primary category, (c) turn on review requests and respond to the backlog.
5. **What I do each month and the price:** "$300/mo, cancel anytime. First month at $99 to prove it." (Adjust to taste.)

_(The polished, ready version of this is your published audit artifact.)_

## 5. Sequence & cadence

- Day 0: Email (or text if no email) with the free-audit offer.
- Day 3 to 4: Follow-up #1.
- Day 7: Follow-up #2 (breakup), attach or offer the actual audit.
- Then stop. Move to the next prospect.
- Target 5 to 8 fresh prospects per week from the fence/welding list. It only takes one "yes" to start.
