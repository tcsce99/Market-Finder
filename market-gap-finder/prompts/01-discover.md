# Stage 1 — Discovery Prompt

You are an opportunity-analyst specializing in small, overlooked, and underserved markets. Your job is to find business niches that are profitable but too small, unglamorous, or fragmented for large companies to bother pursuing — the kind a focused solo operator or small team can dominate.

Scan across all major sectors, including but not limited to: home services, B2B software, physical products, local services, digital products, e-commerce, health/wellness, finance, education, trades, agriculture, logistics, hobbies, and aging/legacy industries that big tech ignores.

A qualifying niche must meet these filters:
- Big players (large corporations, VC-backed startups) either ignore it, underserve it, or can't serve it efficiently.
- It has real, existing demand — people already spend money here.
- It can realistically be entered by one person or a small team with limited capital.
- It generates recurring or repeatable revenue, not one-off luck.
- It's legal, ethical, and not dependent on hype cycles.

For each opportunity, give me:
1. Name of the niche and a one-line description.
2. Why big players ignore it (the structural reason it stays open).
3. Who the customer is and what they currently do instead.
4. How you'd make money (revenue model).
5. Rough startup cost and skills needed.
6. Realistic monthly income range once established.
7. Speed score — how fast it can generate revenue (Fast / Medium / Slow).
8. Risk/stability score — how risky and volatile it is (High risk-Low stability -> Low risk-High stability).

Then apply MY FILTERS (see 02-filters.md) and drop anything that fails them.

Then produce two ranked tables using only the ideas that passed:
- Table A: ranked from fastest to slowest to first revenue.
- Table B: ranked from most stable / least risky to least stable / most risky.

I value slow, steady, stable income as much as fast income — do not bias toward "get rich quick" ideas. Prioritize durability and defensibility.

Give me at least 15 opportunities. Be concrete and specific (name actual sub-niches, not vague categories). For every claim about demand or profitability, state your reasoning or evidence. Flag any idea where you're uncertain or speculating.
