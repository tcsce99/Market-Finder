# Stage 3 — Validation Prompt

Take these 3 opportunities and build me a validation plan for each — a cheap, fast way to prove real demand exists BEFORE I invest serious time or money.

My top 3: [Opportunity 1], [Opportunity 2], [Opportunity 3].

For each one, give me:

1. **The core assumption to test.** What's the single riskiest belief this idea depends on? (Usually "people will actually pay for this.") If that belief is wrong, the whole thing collapses — so we test it first.

2. **A cheap validation experiment.** How do I test that assumption for under [$100] and in under [2 weeks], without building anything real? Think: fake landing pages, manually doing the service for a few people by hand, cold outreach, posting in the right communities, pre-selling before I build. Be specific about what I actually do, step by step.

3. **The signal to watch for.** What exact result tells me "yes, this is real, keep going" vs "no, walk away"? Give me a concrete number or threshold (e.g. "if 5 of 30 people you contact say yes, proceed"). No vague "see if there's interest."

4. **Where my first customers actually are.** Name specific places — websites, forums, subreddits, Facebook groups, directories, local spots — where the buyers for this already hang out. Not "social media." Actual named places.

5. **First-dollar path.** What's the single fastest way to earn the very first real dollar from a real customer, even if it's manual, ugly, and doesn't scale? I want proof of payment, not a business plan.

6. **Kill criteria.** What would make me abandon this idea? Give me clear "if X happens, stop" conditions so I don't waste months on a dud out of stubbornness.

Rank the 3 by which I should test FIRST — factoring in cheapest and fastest to validate, not just biggest upside. I'd rather quickly kill bad ideas and find the one real winner than fall in love with one on paper.

Be blunt. If one of my three picks looks weak, tell me.
